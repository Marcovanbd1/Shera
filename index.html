The moment the system cannot hear

Not at appointments. Not at referrals. Not at records.

But before any of that. The moment when someone notices something is wrong, but does not yet know what to do. They hesitate, minimise, loop, delay, and tell themselves they are not “bad enough yet”.

This is the most common starting point for harm, escalation, and inequality in the NHS, and it is structurally invisible.

What it is

Neighbourhood Signals is a new layer of neighbourhood-level listening and navigation infrastructure. It listens for early signs of uncertainty and unmet need, then turns what it hears into simple, human next steps people can actually use.

1. Neighbourhood-level listening

Privacy-safe signals help understand what a neighbourhood is quietly struggling with. This does not track individuals. It listens to patterns, so teams can respond earlier and more proportionately.

2. Human navigation

For individuals, it offers Support Playlists, small curated sets of local and national support options, designed to reduce cognitive load at the point where people most need steadiness.

How the AI is used

This is not a conversation. The AI does not diagnose, generate medical advice, or make decisions about people.

The model is used for what it is genuinely good at, understanding messy, unstructured language and recognising the shape of someone’s situation. It then matches that to a controlled, curated set of trusted support routes.

The AI interprets. The system retrieves. The person chooses.

Why this is safer than what we currently do

Current systems often assume clarity at the moment of contact. They expect people, at their most uncertain, to act with certainty.

Neighbourhood Signals recognises uncertainty as a safety signal, not a user failure. It provides proportionate navigation before clinical escalation is required.

Why this matters for equity

The people most likely to struggle at the uncertainty edge are also the people most likely to experience poorer outcomes. By making early navigation calmer and easier, Neighbourhood Signals reduces structural disadvantage before it compounds.

Equity is not a downstream adjustment here. It is built into where the system begins to listen.

In the wild

Neighbourhood Signals is designed to travel. Not as a campaign, but as simple, trusted access to support where people already are.

The simple promise

We make finding support easier than staying confused.

Note on boundaries: Neighbourhood Signals is a neighbourhood safety layer. It is not an EPR module, not an AI triage engine, and not a replacement for clinical judgement.
